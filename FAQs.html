FAQs

Questions

Answers

"What languages are spoken on Taniti?"

While Tanitian is the offical national language of the island, many residents in Taniti's urban areas (including employees of our island's hospital) speak fluent English. However, very little English is spoken in rural areas, especially by the older residents.

"What is Taniti's national currency?"

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its currency, but many businesses will also accept euros and yen. Several banks facilitate currency exchange, and many businesses accept major credit cards.

"What voltage are the power outlets on Taniti?"

Power outlets are 120 volts (the same as in the United States).

"What is the drinking age on Taniti?"

Taniti's legal drinking age is 18.

"What are the hours of sale/service of alcohol?"

Alcohol is service is allowed between the hours of 9am - 12:00am; no alcohol may be sold or served after midnight.

"What sort of crime does Taniti have?"

Violent crime on the island is extremely rare; however pickpocketing and other petty crimes are increasing as tourism increases. Please remember to secure your belongings when in public spaces.

"What national holidays does Taniti celebrate?"

See our holiday calendar for an up-to-date listing of our national holiday dates for the current calendar year. Please note that many tourist attractions and resaurants are CLOSED on national holidays, so be sure to plan your visit accordingly.

"Where can I get groceries on the island?"

Taniti has two supermarkets, two smaller grocery stores, and one convenience store that is open 24 hours a day.
